# Gaps

- DINOv2 grounding assumes foreground-background separation (Eq. 25); untested when foreground itself is heavily occluded, deformable, or non-rigid (cloth, fluid, crowded multi-agent scenes). Does the gated residual silently close and revert to DreamerV3 behavior in such regimes?
- The cross-modal residual gate is evaluated only on 64×64 pixel inputs, which yield ~20 ViT-B/14 patches. It is unknown whether grounding quality degrades or improves at higher resolutions (224×224) where DINOv2 was pretrained, and whether CLS-token semantics remain invariant under heavy aliasing at 64×64.
- Trust-region δ_t is updated with τ_EIG and τ_RPL weights, but neither the sensitivity to these coefficients nor their automatic calibration is reported; behavior when EIG and RPL disagree (e.g., overconfident miscalibrated ensemble) is unexamined.
- MC-Dropout substitute for the K=5 ensemble trades 0.14 DFM(1000) on Humanoid-Walk for ~10% compute — but only a single task is reported; the variance-calibration penalty on sparse-reward (Acrobot) or manipulation (Adroit) is untested.
- Theoretical value-gap bound uses IPMs over occupancy measures, but the paper never empirically estimates the IPM term or checks that the bound is non-vacuous on actual trajectories — bound-vs-measured-regret correlation is an open experiment.
- ProprioGIRL's MSAE warm-start failure mode is acknowledged but not characterized: how long must the MSAE train before GIRL activation without destabilizing the actor, and does off-policy MSAE pretraining transfer across tasks?
- All experiments use matched environment-step budgets; whether GIRL's wall-clock disadvantage can ever be recouped in real-robot settings where data is expensive and compute is cheap is not quantified beyond a qualitative statement.
- Distilled Semantic Prior is trained on replay from a single run; student drift under non-stationary replay (new tasks added, policy shifts) is not tested — catastrophic forgetting of DSP during multi-task Meta-World training is possible but unreported.
- No evaluation on partially observable or discrete-action domains (author-stated), and no ablation on the gate's behavior for long-horizon credit assignment beyond horizon 500 — extrapolation to 10^4-step horizons (navigation, locomotion-over-terrain) is speculative.
- DFM is defined only over KL between posterior and prior on real-data-anchored c_{t+1:t+ℓ}; it does not measure semantic hallucination directly. A grounded-truth drift metric (e.g., agent-state reconstruction error against a simulator oracle) is missing.
- Single-author, single-affiliation work with no mentioned code release; reproducibility risk is high for cross-lab replication, especially of the RPL signal which depends on online real-environment KL computation.
- Cross-modal consistency loss L_cm has a fixed weight μ=0.1; interaction between μ and the adaptive β_t is not studied — these can conflict, with the consistency term pulling latents toward DINOv2 semantics while the trust region tightens around the learned prior.
