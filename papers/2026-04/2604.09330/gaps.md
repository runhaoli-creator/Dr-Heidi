# Gaps

- VAG's video branch conditions the action branch but not vice-versa; the authors identify this as wasted control signal. Whether bidirectional conditioning (action→video via injected per-step action tokens or joint cross-attention) actually improves alignment, or whether synchronized denoising already saturates the alignment benefit, is untested.
- Horizon is fixed at 93 frames (~10 s). No closed-loop rollout protocol or error-growth analysis beyond the horizon. LIBERO-Long replay SR is only 42% (vs. 70/72/64 for shorter tasks), suggesting degradation, but the paper never isolates horizon length as a factor.
- The headline real-world result (π0.5 35% → π0.5-w-VAG-pretrain 55%) is based on only 20 trials per condition on a single pick-and-place family with 131 VAG-training samples and 20 VLA-fine-tune samples. Statistical significance, seed/task variance, and scaling behavior of the synthetic-pretrain gain are not characterized.
- No ablation isolates the design choice of adaptive 3D spatiotemporal average pooling vs. alternatives (learnable linear projection, per-timestep token-level cross-attention, Q-former). The chosen embedding dim C''=132 is inherited from Diffusion Policy with no sensitivity study.
- The action U-Net capacity is likely undersized relative to DiT video branch (authors acknowledge a planned DiT upgrade). Whether action modeling quality, not cross-modal alignment, is the current bottleneck is unclear.
- No comparison against modern WA/WM baselines on matched splits — video baselines are generic (SVD, Wan2.2, CP2); WA methods (WorldVLA, GR2, UVA, Motus, DreamGen end-to-end) are discussed only in related work. The claim of "outperforms counterparts" is therefore against IDM-style two-stage pipelines built by the authors, not off-the-shelf WA systems.
- Textual instructions are produced by Qwen2.5-VL auto-captioning during training. Robustness to OOD human phrasing, multi-step instructions, or negations is not probed.
- Success criterion for action prediction (per-dim error < 0.2) is a heuristic and may not correlate with task completion; the relationship between this proxy and real-robot success is not quantified.
- The "detach" on the video latent before feeding into the action branch means video gradients do not receive any action-side feedback. How this affects long-horizon alignment vs. a non-detached variant is unknown.
- All experiments use single-view (head camera) on AgiBot and self-collected data, and dual-view (head+wrist stitched) on LIBERO. Generalization to arbitrary multi-camera rigs and egocentric/exocentric mix is not studied.
