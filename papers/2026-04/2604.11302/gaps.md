# Gaps

- The c2w anchor is updated only by deterministic FK, with no uncertainty over camera extrinsics or joint encoders; robustness under miscalibration, encoder drift, or wrist-mount flex is untested — a principled test would inject calibrated noise into FK and measure degradation of Memory SR vs a Bayesian / particle-filter c2w variant.
- "Lossless spatial memory" assumes occluded objects do not move; there is no experiment with a dynamic scene (pushed by other agents, gravity, or a second arm). Need a benchmark where targets shift during occlusion to see whether the anchor helps or actively misleads.
- Full perception pipeline (InSpatio-WorldFM + Florence-2) was abandoned for a geometric oracle; the paper proposes JEPA-similarity (LeWM/DINOv2) and depth-augmented scorers as Phase 2 but reports zero numbers. A direct comparison of {geometric oracle, Florence-2, DINOv2 cosine, Depth-Anything-V2 + RGB} scorers on E3 would quantify whether the architecture actually survives without the oracle.
- MCTS variants lose 0.29 absolute SR on non-memory steps vs greedy; a hybrid System-1/System-2 controller that falls back to reactive action when the target is directly observable is a concrete, testable improvement.
- The monotone kinematic-distance reward (Eq. 4) only fits reach tasks; extension to contact-rich manipulation (grasp, insertion, pouring) where reward is non-monotone in d_3D has not been attempted and would require a different scorer interface.
- Only D=1 and D=2 are tested with fixed B=4, 10 actions/step, 2 s budget; a compute-scaling study (D up to 4+, branching up to 16, or AlphaZero-style visit-count policies) would clarify whether the 17% depth-lookahead gain is a ceiling or continues to grow.
- Render latency ~2.4 s/frame limits the full pipeline to ~5 nodes per cycle — well below D=2,B=4 theoretical 20. Even with the proposed LeWM switch, no wall-clock ceiling for real-time manipulation (≤50 ms control loops) is characterised.
- No real-robot transfer: MuJoCo InSpatio renders hide sim-to-real gaps in lighting, texture, and kinematic noise. A minimum-viable Franka E3 replication is needed before architectural claims generalise.
- Multi-robot Dec-MCTS extension is sketched but not prototyped; the question of how to merge conflicting c2w observations of the same object from two robots with different calibration is open.
- α=0.7 reference-latent blend was tuned on a single held-out trajectory; robustness across tasks, camera viewpoints, and robot embodiments is unknown — a sensitivity sweep or learned gating (predict α per step from observation-prediction residual) would make the method deployment-ready.
- Anchor drift is mitigated but never measured directly; the paper reports Phase 0 SSIM=1.0 on 5 trajectories but no long-horizon (20+ step) drift curve for the blended update, leaving an empirical gap in the core "persistent" claim.
